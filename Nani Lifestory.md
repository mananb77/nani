# **Krishna Bhargava: A Life Story**

**September 7th, 1940 \- December 4th, 2021**

## **Early Life in Bikaner**

Krishna Bhargava, affectionately known as "Nani," was born on **September 7th, 1940**, on the auspicious day of Janmashtami in Bikaner, Rajasthan. She was the youngest and most pampered child in a family of four siblings—three sisters and one brother.

### **Family Heritage**

* **Father**: Shankarlayal Bhargava (Doctor)  
* **Family Legacy**: A distinguished family of doctors and army fighters  
* **Historical Significance**: Family photos are displayed in the Bikaner museum to this day, testament to their well-educated and prominent status  
* **Privileged Upbringing**: Nani lived like a "princess" with royal treatment, an ambassador car, and people bringing food to her home (which embarrassed her)  
* **Freedom Fighter Connection**: Her mother was a freedom fighter during India's independence movement  
* **Military Heritage**: Her uncle (tauji) was a commander in the British army during World War 2

## **Athletic Excellence**

Nani was an exceptional athlete who broke barriers for women in sports:

### **Championships & Achievements**

* **State Field Hockey Team** member  
* **Table Tennis State Champion**  
* Coached by **Dhyanchand**, India's legendary hockey player  
* Known for her signature deceptive playing style: looking one direction while hitting the ball another  
* Consistently beat everyone in badminton

## **Education: A Pioneer**

At a time when girls were actively discouraged from pursuing education, Nani defied societal norms:

* Attended an all-girls school in Bikaner  
* Earned a **Master's degree in Hindi & Sanskrit**  
* Her educational achievement was remarkable for women of her generation

## **Marriage & Family Life**

**Married**: May 1st, 1962

Nani and Nana (her husband) built a beautiful life together, raising two daughters:

* **Anshu** (Aparna)  
* **Bebu**

They were adventurous spirits who explored the world together, visiting:

* Singapore  
* Disney World, USA  
* Niagara Falls  
* Vaishnodevi  
* Kodaikanal  
* Mussoorie  
* Haridwar  
* Shaurya Smarak

### **Golden Anniversary**

The couple celebrated their **50th wedding anniversary** surrounded by family and friends in a grand celebration.

## **Life in Bhopal**

### **The BHEL Community**

Nani became an integral part of the BHEL (Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited) community in Bhopal, where she:

* Won **multiple awards** at the BHEL Ladies Club  
* Built lasting friendships  
* Became known for her hospitality and social grace

### **The Perfect Home**

Nani was a perfectionist who maintained an immaculate household:

**Garden Excellence**

* Designed the entire garden layout herself  
* Created beautiful flower arrangements  
* Served as the family's interior designer

**The Bhopal House**

* Spotlessly clean and organized at all times  
* Beautifully decorated living room  
* Well-maintained car  
* Large mandir (prayer room) in the home

## **Nani's Best Qualities**

### **Creative & Welcoming**

* Made everyone feel at ease  
* Had the talent to talk with anyone  
* Very creative in all her endeavors  
* Warm and welcoming personality

### **Master Chef & Hostess**

**Famous Culinary Skills**:

* Known for huge spreads on the table  
* **Expert pressure cooker handler**: Had the first pressure cooker; all friends came to learn from her  
* Pioneer in using solar cooker  
* Famous for **lachhe ka achaar** and **katthal ka achaar** (pickles)  
* Signature homemade ketchup  
* Specialties: Paneer paratha rolls, cutlets, samosas, bread rolls  
* Tricolored sandwiches with Kissan jam and Amul butter  
* Delicious Gulab Jamun

**Party Hosting**:

* Best Anshu & Aparna's birthday parties  
* Excellent cakes and decorations  
* Famous chaat parties

### **Sewing & Crafts**

* Stitched amazing dresses for Ankita's 1st birthday  
* Made Manan's chair seat cover and sweater  
* Skilled seamstress with attention to detail

### **Spiritual & Philosophical**

* Went to mandir every Monday  
* Had a large mandir in her home  
* Well-read in India's greatest philosophical texts  
* Deep spiritual connection

## **Summer Vacations & Family Traditions**

### **The Bhopal Hub**

Every summer, children from all families would gather at Nani and Nana's home in Bhopal:

* Nani cooked enormous amounts of food  
* All kids went to the swimming pool  
* Famous for **aloo ki paratha with aamrus** (mango juice)

### **Monday Picnics**

A beloved tradition:

* Changed clothes in the car and drove straight to Kerwa Dam  
* Created lasting family memories  
* Adventures at Haridwar's children's park with ducks  
* The memorable incident where Nani fell into the water while stepping on stones

## **Fun & Games**

### **Entertainment**

* Expert at playing thash and courtpiece (card games)

### **Childhood Mischief**

Nani was blessed with multiple grandchildren who brought her immense joy. She was present for:

The Bhopal extended family group remained close throughout the years, with regular gatherings and celebrations.

## **A Life Well-Lived**

Krishna Bhargava lived through momentous times in Indian history—from British rule and World War 2, through independence, to modern India. She witnessed and contributed to:

* India's independence movement (through her mother)  
* The growth of education for women  
* Women's participation in sports  
* The development of modern India

**Her Lasting Impact**:

* Pioneer for women's education and sports  
* Exceptional homemaker and cook  
* Loving grandmother  
* Devoted wife of 59+ years  
* Community pillar in the BHEL family  
* Master gardener and interior designer  
* Storyteller and entertainer  
* Source of warmth and welcome for all who knew her
